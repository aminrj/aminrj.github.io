<div class="magnet-contents">
  <h2>What's Inside</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>
      <strong>1. Detection and Alerting</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">You cannot contain what you cannot detect. AI-specific incidents require monitoring that traditional SOC tools do not provide.</div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>2. Containment and Isolation</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">Containment for AI agents is not just about network isolation. It involves stopping autonomous actions, preserving evidence, and preventing behavioral drift.</div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>3. Response and Recovery</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">Response to AI incidents requires different procedures than traditional incidents. The agent's probabilistic behavior means recovery is not simply restoring a backup.</div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>4. Communication and Escalation</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">AI incidents often require communication paths that do not exist in traditional incident response plans.</div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>5. Continuous Improvement</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">Containment capability is not a static state. It degrades over time as agents evolve, new attack patterns emerge, and team members change.</div>
    </li>
  </ul>
</div>
