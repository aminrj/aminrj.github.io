<div class="magnet-contents">
  <h2>What's Inside</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>
      <strong>1. Governance and Policy</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">Every AI agent needs a defined owner, a clear scope, and documented boundaries. Without governance, agents operate in the blind spots between teams.</div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>2. Risk and Compliance Mapping</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">AI agents introduce risk categories that do not fit neatly into existing frameworks. Map them explicitly before deployment.</div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>3. Organizational Capability and Skills</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">The people who build, operate, and respond to AI agents need skills that most security programs do not currently develop.</div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>4. Procurement and Vendor Management</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">When you procure AI capabilities, you are acquiring not just technology but behavior, data access patterns, and risk profiles.</div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>5. Operational Controls and Monitoring</strong>
      <div class="magnet-why">An agent in production needs the same operational controls as any other system, plus AI-specific monitoring for probabilistic behavior.</div>
    </li>
  </ul>
